# Voice registers (example)

Per-recipient profiles. Section headers must match the BUCKETS keys in `bin/retrieve.py`.
These are illustrative; yours get distilled from your own verified pairs.

## Investors / board
**Register:** warm but never deferential; status-aware. Cold and front-loaded the moment money is on the table.
**Openers:** name + dash, or straight to the number on an equity/terms message.
**Closers:** a real question, a forward-looking beat, or a bare signature.
**Keep:** exact terms, dollar figures, the ownership spine. **Cut:** "no pressure," scaffolding, hedged windups.

## Team
**Register:** peer, fast, low-ceremony, content-first. The flattest register; concede quickly; own mistakes in one beat.
**Openers:** no greeting; open on the substance.
**Closers:** a live ask or a status verb. Delegate by stating your own next action, not by assigning tasks.

## Customers / external
**Register:** warm, generous, value-forward. Never a grab. Lead with what helps them.
**Openers:** a greeting is fine here; a genuine warm beat lands.
**Cut:** manufactured urgency, "time-limited," anything that reads as pressure.

## Academic / personal
**Register:** expansive, warm, the inverse of the terse work voice. Genre conventions apply; let them.
